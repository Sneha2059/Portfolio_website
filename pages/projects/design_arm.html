6-DOF Industrial Robotic Arm

Design and counterbalance mechanism for efficient manufacturing

SolidWorks Mechanical Design Counterbalance Mechanism Industrial Automation

Project Overview

This project involved the design of a 6-degree-of-freedom industrial robotic arm with an innovative counterbalance mechanism (CBM) to reduce actuator requirements. The arm features RPPPYR configuration (R - Roll, P - Pitch, Y - Yaw) and incorporates a spring-based counterbalance system to minimize gravitational torque on joints.

Complete 6-DOF Robotic Arm Assembly

Design Features

The design underwent multiple iterations to accommodate the counterbalance mechanism while maintaining manufacturability. Key features include:

  • Spring counterbalance mechanism reduces actuator torque requirements by 68-86%
  • Actuators for joints 2, 3, and 4 mounted at the base to reduce gravitational torque
  • Double parallelogram mechanism maintains reference planes for each joint
  • Compact circular base design housing all primary components
  • GT2 timing belts and 3D-printed pulleys for power transmission

Counterbalance Mechanism

The counterbalance system uses springs, wires, and pulleys with a double parallelogram mechanism to maintain reference axes for each joint. This innovative approach allows each joint to independently compensate for gravitational torque without being affected by other joints' movements.

The mechanism reduced required torque at joint 2 by 86.5% (from 64 kgf.cm to 8.6 kgf.cm) and at joint 3 by 68% (from 25 kgf.cm to 8 kgf.cm), enabling the use of smaller, more cost-effective actuators.
