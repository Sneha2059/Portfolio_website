DESIGN, FABRICATION AND AUTOMATION OF INDUSTRIAL 6-DOF ROBOTIC ARM WITH ON-DEMAND MANUFACTURING CAPABILITIES FOR MASS/BATCH PRODUCTION INDUSTRIES

Final year thesis project (Undergrad)

Citation :

Poudel, Sneha & Timsina, Sameer & Chaudhari, Abishek & Sah, Gangotri. (2024). DESIGN, FABRICATION AND AUTOMATION OF INDUSTRIAL 6-DOF ROBOTIC ARM WITH ON-DEMAND MANUFACTURING CAPABILITIES FOR MASS/BATCH PRODUCTION INDUSTRIES. 10.13140/RG.2.2.12232.43522.

Abstract

Industry 4.0, the fourth industrial revolution, combines digital technologies and au- tomation in manufacturing. This project aims to design and control a six-degree - of freedom robotic arm with counterbalance mechanism and it also incorporates the con- cept of on-demand manufacturing and uses the model robotic arm to integrate it.

The project seeks to enhance manufacturing operations, improve efficiency, and increase productivity. The robotic arm will be driven by servo and stepper motors controlled by Raspberry Pi B+ microcontroller. Real-time communication and control will optimize the manufacturing process, leading to streamlined operations and increased responsive- ness. Additionally, weight reduction and counterbalancing mechanism has been imple- mented to ensure use of cost effective actuators.

This project has the potential to trans- form the manufacturing industry by enabling direct communication between clients and manufacturers, eliminating intermediaries and revolutionizing manufacturing pro- cesses.

Objective of the project

  • Streamline the order process by developing a client-side website that enables real- time order placement, provides comprehensive product information, and offers a login system for order tracking.
  • Enhance order management and real-time tracking capabilities by creating a software program that integrates with user and order database.
  • Enhance inventory control by implementing software to track and monitor each material in storage, facilitating proactive inventory management.
  • Design and Fabrication of 6-DOF Robotic Arm with spring counter-balancing mechanism within the body.
  • Simulation of kinematics of the robotic arm within the MATLAB environment.

Software and Hardware requirements

  • Raspberry Pi OS
  • Simulation and design softwares : SolidWorks, Matlab, Ansys
  • Python with modules : streamlit, sqlalchemy, RPi.GPIO
  • Raspberry Pi
  • NEMA 23 and NEMA 17 Stepper Motor
  • 5V 2A DC Power Supply
  • MG996R Servo Motor
  • SG90 Micro Servo Motor etc.
