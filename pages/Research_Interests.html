Research Interests

Research Statement

My journey as a researcher and engineer has been driven by a deep curiosity for bridging the gap between traditional engineering and cutting-edge AI/ML. Starting in robotics, I worked with control systems and hardware designs that often lacked scalability, pushing me toward data-driven approaches to solve complex engineering challenges. This exploration led me to AI/ML, where I discovered the immense potential of machine learning in transforming engineering design, particularly in data-driven design, scientific machine learning (Sci-ML), and geometric deep learning.

Currently, as a Research Intern at Autolab Tech (soon transitioning to a full-time role), I am investigating graph neural networks (GNNs) for classifying and retrieving 3D-CAD models directly from STEP files. Unlike mesh-based representations, STEP files preserve precise geometric and topological information, making them ideal for engineering applications. My work focuses on developing robust AI models that can interpret these complex structures without information loss, paving the way for smarter CAD retrieval systems and generative design tools. Looking ahead, I aspire to deepen my research in data-driven design and AI-augmented engineering, particularly Scientific Machine Learning (Sci-ML) for Engineering which helps in leveraging physics-informed neural networks (PINNs) and hybrid models to enhance simulation, optimization, and inverse design, Geometric Deep Learning for Expanding the use of GNNs, transformers, and diffusion models for structured 3D engineering data, improving generative design and automated manufacturing, and AI/ML at the Engineering Intersection which works on developing interpretable, real-world applicable AI systems that integrate seamlessly with industrial workflows.

To advance this research, I aim to collaborate with leading experts in the field, where interdisciplinary AI/ML research is thriving. I am eager to contribute to and learn from academic environments that emphasize practical, impactful research at the intersection of AI and engineering.

Despite hailing from a country with limited developmental resources, I have consistently dared to dream ambitiously. My commitment to excellence is evident in the diligent efforts I have invested thus far, and I remain steadfast in my resolve to persevere and excel, given the appropriate opportunity.

My ultimate goal is to push the boundaries of data-driven engineering, creating AI systems that not only enhance design automation but also foster innovation in manufacturing, socio-technical systems, robotics, and beyond.
