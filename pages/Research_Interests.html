Research Interests

Research Statement

My journey as a researcher and engineer has been driven by a deep curiosity for understanding and advancing continuum mechanics and multiscale modeling, while leveraging modern computational tools to address engineering challenges. Starting in robotics, I worked with control systems and hardware designs that often lacked scalability, which motivated me to explore data-driven approaches. Over time, I realized the potential of combining traditional engineering methods with AI/ML as auxiliary tools to accelerate and enhance modeling, simulation, and design processes.

Currently, as a Research Intern at Autolab Tech (soon transitioning to a full-time role), I am investigating graph neural networks (GNNs) for classifying and retrieving 3D-CAD models directly from STEP files. STEP files preserve precise geometric and topological information, making them valuable for engineering applications. My work aims to develop efficient computational methods that interpret such complex structures, contributing to smarter CAD retrieval systems and generative design.

My research interest lies at the intersection of multiscale modeling, continuum mechanics, and artificial intelligence. I aim to develop hybrid frameworks that integrate physics-based models with AI to bridge scales from microstructural phenomena to macroscopic material behavior. Traditional continuum and multiscale methods, though powerful, often face limitations in computational cost and generalization; AI offers a transformative tool to accelerate simulations, learn complex constitutive laws, and embed physical constraints through approaches like physics-informed neural networks and surrogate models. By combining these paradigms, my goal is to create robust, interpretable, and computationally efficient models that enhance predictive capabilities in engineering applications such as material design, structural analysis, and biomechanics, ultimately contributing to more reliable and scalable solutions for real-world challenges.
